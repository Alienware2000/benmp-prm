# Global Crusade Partners Platform

Client discussion brief for BENMP and the Healing Jesus Campaign.

## 1. What We Are Building

We are building an internal Partner Relationship Management platform for the BENMP office.

This is not a public app that partners need to download or learn. Partners can continue giving and communicating through familiar channels such as Mobile Money, card links, bank transfer, WhatsApp, SMS, email, and phone calls.

The platform is for BENMP staff. It should help the office keep partner records, track giving, acknowledge gifts, manage follow-up, and report clearly.

In practical terms, staff should be able to open one system and see who gave, who needs to be thanked, who needs a call, who is active, who missed a month, which gifts need review, and what leadership needs to know.

The platform should feel like an operating console for the BENMP office: partner records, giving records, statement imports, thank-you drafts, follow-up tasks, prayer requests, country or region reports, and safe AI assistance in one place.

## 2. How The Monthly Partner Cycle Works

The system should be built around the monthly BENMP partnership rhythm.

1. Before or during the month, partners can be reminded through approved messages.
2. A partner gives through an approved channel.
3. The system receives a payment notification, or staff imports a statement record.
4. The gift is verified through a provider confirmation, statement record, or office-reviewed manual entry.
5. The system matches the gift to an existing partner, or sends it to a review queue if the giver is unclear.
6. The verified gift is added to the partner's giving history.
7. A thank-you message is drafted for WhatsApp, SMS, email, or phone follow-up.
8. Important cases are flagged, such as new partners, missed months, large gifts, yearly active partners, prayer requests, and unresolved records.
9. At month end, leadership can see who gave, who did not, who is active, how much came in, and what follow-up is needed.

The important principle is that a gift should only become final when there is trustworthy evidence. Payment notifications, bank or Mobile Money statements, and office-reviewed entries can all be used, but unsupported claims should not become final giving records by themselves.

## 3. What The First Version Includes

The first version should be practical, focused, and usable by the office.

Partner records: Name, mobile number, WhatsApp number, email, country, city, church, partner status, giving frequency, preferred communication method, prayer requests, notes, and assigned staff owner.

Giving records: Gift date, amount, currency, payment method, campaign or crusade supported, total giving, last gift date, active-year status, and high-attention status.

Giving intake: Payment notifications where possible, plus statement import where automatic notification is not possible.

Reconciliation queue: A review area for gifts that cannot be confidently matched to a partner.

Acknowledgement workflow: Thank-you drafts after giving, with staff review before sending.

Follow-up work: Tasks for calls, missed months, unusually large gifts, prayer needs, new partners, and unresolved records.

Dashboard and reports: Total partners, new partners, monthly giving, active partners, giving by country or region, high-attention gifts, unresolved records, and follow-up work.

AI support: Safe AI assistance that can answer questions from trusted data and help draft messages. AI should not send messages or change important records without approval.

## 4. What Success Looks Like In The First Demo

The first serious demo should prove the full relationship loop with real or test data.

1. Staff can log in securely.
2. Existing partner records can be imported.
3. A test gift can be recorded manually or imported from a payment CSV and matched to a partner.
4. The gift can be matched to a partner, or sent to review if the giver is unclear.
5. A thank-you draft can be created.
6. Active-year and high-attention flags can be applied.
7. Follow-up tasks can be assigned.
8. A statement import can show how bank, wallet, or Mobile Money records enter the system.
9. Leadership can see the result on a simple dashboard.
10. The AI assistant can answer basic questions from the same data the dashboard uses.

If this loop works, BENMP will have the foundation for a real live system rather than a disconnected mockup.

## 5. What BENMP Needs To Provide

Some items are needed immediately. Others can come later, but they should be identified early.

Needed for the first serious build:

1. Existing partner list or benmp.com export, preferably in Excel or CSV format.
2. Sample giving records or sample statements, with sensitive details removed if needed.
3. The official giving channels that should be represented first.
4. The current Mobile Money, bank, wallet, or card-giving setup and who owns each account.
5. A small initial staff list with roles, such as administrator, finance, communications, follow-up, and viewer.
6. Region or country grouping preferences for reports.
7. A few examples of the tone BENMP wants for thank-you messages and partner updates.

Useful soon, but not required to start:

1. Final WhatsApp Business ownership details.
2. Final SMS sender and email sender details.
3. Full historical giving exports.
4. Complete crusade or campaign records.
5. Final approval rules for large message batches.

## 6. Decisions Needed Now

These are the decisions that affect the first build most directly.

1. Which existing partner and giving data should be imported first?
2. Which giving channels should be represented first in the system?
3. Can the office export Mobile Money, bank, wallet, or remittance statements regularly?
4. Who should be included in the first staff login list, and what role should each person have?
5. Should reporting start with the region blocks Ghana, Rest of Africa, Europe, UK, America, South America, and Australia/Asia, or should BENMP use a different grouping?
6. What are the most important dashboard questions leadership wants answered first?

The remaining details can be refined after the first workflow is proven. For example, AI rules, advanced messaging approvals, regional coordinator permissions, and high-volume WhatsApp automation do not need to delay the first build.

## 7. What Comes Later

The first version should not try to become everything at once.

Later phases can add live WhatsApp and SMS sending, automated reminder sequences, regional coordinator access, deeper month-end reports, testimony and crusade update workflows, and more advanced AI support. (Money is reconciled from a periodic payment CSV; a live payment integration is only added if the office later asks for in-app instant confirmation.) The first priority is the foundation: trusted partner records, trusted giving records, timely acknowledgement, clear follow-up, useful reporting, and a workflow the BENMP office can actually use.
